The market has drawn a clear line around $2,800. Below it, properties lease in days. Above it, they sit for months.

That pattern is the most important takeaway in this analysis. The three fastest leases all landed between $2,650 and $2,775 and rented in 26 days or less. The moment the rent crosses into the high $2,900s, days-on-market jumps to 150+. The two active listings at $3,000 and $3,175 have been sitting 68 and 79 days respectively, still not rented — a leading indicator that the upper band is currently being rejected by the renter pool.

How Della Scala stacks up — the trade-offs

The subject property has genuine strengths and one real soft spot worth naming honestly:

Strengths (+)

  • Point Cypress is a premium Dr Phillips enclave — superior location vs. Toscana
  • Show-ready condition with no friction during showings
  • No competing units currently available in the immediate Point Cypress neighborhood
  • Late May into summer is peak rental season in Orlando

Soft Spots (−)

  • Carpeted throughout the main living areas (kitchen and baths only have hard floors)
  • Renters at this Dr Phillips price tier generally expect hard floors throughout
  • Carpet can be a daily walk-through eliminator — renters tour, see carpet in the great room, and mentally pass

The location premium and the show-ready condition roughly offset the carpet limitation. The low inventory in the immediate neighborhood is the most meaningful tailwind — it creates room to test a slightly higher price than the comps alone would suggest.
